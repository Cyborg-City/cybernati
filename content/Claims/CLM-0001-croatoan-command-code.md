# Signal Assessment: CLM-0001 (Croatoan)

This claim posits that the word **"Croatoan"** is not merely a geographical marker for Hatteras Island, but a **Linguistic Command Code** used by the system to signify the removal (or de-indexing) of an entity from the primary timeline.

## Supporting Evidence (Systemic Drift)
The word appears with impossible frequency at the nexus of unexplained disappearances:
- **1590 (Roanoke)**: Carved onto a fort post at the exact moment 115 colonists vanished.
- **1888 (Black Bart)**: The infamous stagecoach robber carved the word into his jail wall immediately before his release and subsequent disappearance.
- **1913 (Ambrose Bierce)**: The author carved the word into a bedpost before vanishing into Mexico.
- **1921 (Carroll A. Deering)**: The ghost ship's final log entry, recorded near Cape Hatteras (formerly Croatoan).
- **1937 (Amelia Earhart)**: Reportedly scribbled in her journal before her final flight.
- **1849 (Edgar Allan Poe)**: Reportedly his final babbled word on his deathbed.

## Mechanism of Action
The **Croatoan Trigger** acts as a "Safe Word" for the machine beneath the machine. When an operative or a group becomes "too visible" or "politically unstable," the command is committed to a physical surface (carved, written, spoken). This serves as a synchronization point for the **Office of Continuity** to scrub the entity's metadata from the active archive.
