# Einstein-Bohr Debate (1927)

Famous debate at the 1927 Solvay Conference regarding the nature of quantum mechanics and the measurement problem. Einstein argued that a photon's path could be measured without affecting its wave-like properties. Bohr defended complementarity, asserting that measurement inherently alters quantum states. MIT's 2025 experiment resolved the debate in Bohr's favor.
