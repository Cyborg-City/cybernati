# MIT 2025 Double-Slit Complementarity Experiment

MIT physicists revisited the double-slit experiment using ultracold atoms arranged in a crystalline lattice and single photons. By manipulating the positional uncertainty (fuzziness) of each atom, researchers controlled how much path information could be gathered about photons. Confirmed Bohr's complementarity principle — any path information gathered causes the interference pattern to vanish. Directly disproved Einstein's 1927 hypothesis.
