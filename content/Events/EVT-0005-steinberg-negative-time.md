# Steinberg Negative Time Measurements

University of Toronto experiments by Steinberg and Sinclair measuring how long transmitted photons spend inside a rubidium atom cloud. Found that atoms spend negative time in their excited state when a photon is transmitted — less time than if no photon had passed at all. The same negative quantity appears across multiple independent measurement definitions (delay time and dwell time), suggesting deeper physical meaning beyond classical causality.
