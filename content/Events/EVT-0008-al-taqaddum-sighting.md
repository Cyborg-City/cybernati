# Al Taqaddum Sighting (2017)

**Date**: October 2017
**Location**: Al Taqaddum Air Base, Iraq

<video controls width="640" height="360"><source src="https://archive.org/download/declassified-aaro/DOD_110956846_Al_Taqaddum_Object.mp4" type="video/mp4"></video>

## Summary
The incident involved a 17-minute continuous track of an unidentified object by a Force Protection Aerostat. The object displayed a characteristic "jellyfish" silhouette with multiple dangling appendages.

## Technical Details
- **Sensor**: Raytheon MTS-A or similar EO/IR system on a moored aerostat.
- **Spectrum**: Infrared only. naked-eye observers and optical cameras reportedly could not locate the object.
- **Trans-Medium Capability**: The footage allegedly includes a segment where the object submerges into a lake and later emerges at a high rate of speed.

## Strategic Redaction
In January 2024, following the public release of the footage by Jeremy Corbell, the Pentagon's **AARO** dismissed the object as a cluster of balloons. Sensor operators present during the event have reportedly challenged this claim, noting the object's stable structure and anomalous movement against prevailing winds.
