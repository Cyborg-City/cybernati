# Field Intelligence Summary: The Roanoke Sequence

Analysis of multiple signals regarding the 1587-1590 disappearance of the Roanoke Island settlers. This sequence represents a fundamental shift in colonial observability.

## 1. Timeline of Disappearance
- **1587**: Establishment of the colony by Sir Walter Raleigh. John White (Governor) departs for England.
- **1587-1590**: White is delayed for 3 years due to the Anglo-Spanish War.
- **August 1590**: White returns. The settlement is dismantled, not destroyed. 
- **Signage**: The word "Croatoan" is found on a fort post; "Cro" on a tree. No "cross" symbol (the pre-arranged distress signal) was found.

## 2. Archaeological Breakthroughs (2012-2025)
- **La Virginia Pars Map**: Backlighting revealed a hidden, patched-over fort symbol 50 miles inland at the confluence of the Roanoke and Chowan rivers.
- **Site X & Site Y (Bertie County)**: Discovery of 16th-century English utilitarian pottery (Surrey-Hampshire Border Ware).
- **The "Negative Signal"**: Complete absence of clay tobacco pipes at Site X confirms the artifacts pre-date the 1607 Jamestown era.
- **Hatteras Island (Croatoan)**: 2024-2025 excavations revealed "cohabitation pits" where English tools (rapier hilt, European copper) were mixed intimately with Native American remains.

## 3. Genetic Continuity (The Lost Colony DNA Project)
- **The Brown Lineage**: DNA analysis has linked modern participants (e.g., "Mr. Brown") to specific 16th-century English markers matching the 1587 Roanoke manifest (Henry and William Brown).
- **Assimilation Mechanism**: The data proves that while the *English Colony* as a political entity vanished, the *Biological Hardware* integrated into local populations (Lumbee and Croatan descendants).

## 4. Institutional Anomalies
- **The Walsingham Sabotage**: Internal theory suggests Sir Francis Walsingham (ORG-0001) intentionally stranded the colony to sabotage Sir Walter Raleigh's royal patent.
- **De-indexing Trigger**: The word "Croatoan" has appeared in subsequent disappearances (Black Bart, Ambrose Bierce, Edgar Allan Poe), suggesting a recurring systemic command.
