# The Roanoke Mystery

In 1587, 115 English colonists led by John White established a settlement on Roanoke Island. By 1590, the colony had completely vanished.

## Timeline
- **1587-07**: Landing of 115 colonists.
- **1587-08-18**: Birth of Virginia Dare, first English child born in the Americas.
- **1587-08-25**: John White returns to England for supplies.
- **1588-1590**: White's return delayed by the Anglo-Spanish War.
- **1590-08**: White returns to Roanoke; finds the settlement abandoned and dismantled.

## Physical Evidence
- **The "Croatoan" Post**: The word "Croatoan" carved into a wooden post of the fort's entrance.
- **The "Cro" Tree**: The letters "Cro" carved into a tree.
- **Dismantled Fort**: The houses were taken down, replaced by a "well built defensible fort."
- **The "La Virginia Pars" Map**: Contains a hidden four-pointed fort symbol under a patch of paper, located 50 miles inland at the confluence of the Roanoke and Chowan Rivers.
- **Visual Evidence**: Access via British Museum Collection Online, Search ID: `1906,0509.1.3` (Imaging reveals the fort symbol under transmitted light).
- **Archaeological Finds (Site X/Y)**: Fragments of English utilitarian pottery known as **Surrey-Hampshire Border Ware** (greenish-glazed ceramic) found in Bertie County. The absence of tobacco pipes confirms the pre-1607 date.
- **Archaeological Finds (Hatteras)**: Elizabethan gold signet ring and sword hilt found in proximity to native remains and cohabitation pits.
