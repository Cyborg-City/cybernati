# The Assimilation & Migration Pattern

Recent evidence suggests the Roanoke colonists did not "vanish" but transitioned from colonizers to refugees through a two-pronged survival strategy.

## 1. Inland Migration (The Mainland Strategy)
- **Target**: Confluence of Roanoke and Chowan rivers (Bertie County).
- **Evidence**: The hidden fort on John White's map; Elizabethan pottery at Site X and Site Y.
- **Status**: Validated by the First Colony Foundation.

## 2. Coastal Assimilation (The Hatteras Strategy)
- **Target**: Croatoan (modern-day Hatteras Island).
- **Evidence**: "Croatoan" clue; Elizabethan sword hilt and gold ring found in native refuse pits; cohabitation pits showing shared living spaces.
- **Oral Tradition**: Lumbee and Croatan descendants maintain histories of intermarriage and assimilation.
- **Genetic Link**: The **Lost Colony DNA Project** (led by Roberta Estes) has linked modern families, such as the Browns, to Tudor-era English lineages (matching names like Henry and William Brown from the 1587 manifest).

## Systemic Anomaly
The persistence of the "Lost Colony" myth despite clear geographical clues ("Croatoan") suggests a systemic de-indexing of successful native-European integration in favor of a "spooky mystery" narrative.
