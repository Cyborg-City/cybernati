# Space Animal Hypothesis

The **Space Animal Hypothesis** posits that the unidentified objects observed in our atmosphere are biological entities rather than mechanical spacecraft.

## Core Concepts
- **Sky Jellyfish**: Kenneth Arnold described some of his later sightings as looking like "transparent jellyfish."
- **Atmospheric Ecology**: The theory suggests that the upper atmosphere is an ecosystem as rich as the ocean, containing life forms that remain mostly invisible or "un-indexed" by standard human observation.
- **Mass Groups**: Arnold argued in 1962 that UFOs are "groups and masses of living organisms" that are a natural part of our atmosphere.

## Strategic Significance
This hypothesis represents a radical pivot from the "nuts and bolts" extraterrestrial narrative. It shifts the phenomenon from a "War of the Worlds" framing to a "Natural History" framing, potentially bypassing institutional redaction by reclassifying the objects as "unidentified wildlife."

## Potential Biological Candidates
- **[[PHE-0001-jellyfish-uap]]**: The 2017 Iraq footage provides the most compelling modern visual evidence for a biological-type anomaly fitting Arnold's "transparent jellyfish" description.
