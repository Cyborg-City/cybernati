# John White

John White was an English artist and mapmaker who served as the governor of the second attempt to found the Roanoke Colony in 1587.

## Key Actions
- **1587 Expedition**: Appointed governor by Sir Walter Raleigh.
- **Artistic Record**: Produced watercolor maps and drawings of the New World (including "La Virginia Pars").
- **Departure**: Left his family (including daughter Eleanor Dare and granddaughter Virginia Dare) to seek supplies in England.
- **1590 Search**: Led the failed search mission that discovered the abandoned settlement.

## Significance
White's mapmaking skills provided the hidden clue (the "patch") that suggests the colonists' planned inland relocation. The 2012 discovery of the fort symbol under a paper patch (revealed by transmitted light at the British Museum) has shifted the focus of Roanoke archaeology to the mainland.
