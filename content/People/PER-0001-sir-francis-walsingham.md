# Dossier: PER-0001 (Sir Francis Walsingham)

Sir Francis Walsingham served as the Principal Secretary to Queen Elizabeth I and is the primary subject of the **Sabotage Theory** regarding the Roanoke Colony.

## Operational Strategy: The Raleigh Neutralization
- **Political Objective**: Sabotage Sir Walter Raleigh's influence. Raleigh held a royal patent for all land he settled. If the colony failed or "disappeared," his claim to power and wealth in the New World was neutralized.
- **The Three-Year Delay**: While the Anglo-Spanish War provided a convenient excuse, Walsingham's control over the English naval and intelligence apparatus allowed him to prioritize other military efforts, effectively ensuring no relief reached Roanoke between 1587 and 1590.
- **Administrative Erasure**: By removing the colony from the official records and allowing it to be absorbed by native populations, Walsingham achieved a "Clean De-indexing"—the people lived, but the political asset was destroyed.

## Legacy and Influence
Walsingham established the **Secretariat** (ORG-0001) as a permanent observability hub. His methods of using "High Strangeness" (the Croatoan signal) and "Administrative Neglect" remain standard operating procedures for the Cybernati™ Legacy Desk.
